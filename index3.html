What is Solar System

A solar system is the collection of planets, asteroids, and moons (moons orbit the planets) that occupy a space around a sun and are held within the gravitational pull of the Sun.

The Solar System is the gravitationally bound system of the Sun and the objects that orbit it , either directly or indirectly. Of the objects that orbit the Sun directly, the largest are the eight planets, with the remainder being smaller objects, the dwarf planets and small Solar System bodies.

    • The 8 plants in our solar system

    1. The smallest and fastest planet, Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and whips around it every 88 Earth days.
    2. Venus is the second planet from the Sun. It is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty.
    3. Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only astronomical object known to harbour and support life
    4. Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and the second-smallest planet in the Solar System, being larger than only Mercury.
    5. Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the Solar System.
    6. Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in the Solar System, after Jupiter.
    7. Uranus has a very unique rotation--it spins on its side at an almost 90-degree angle, unlike other planets.
    8. Neptune is now the most distant planet and is a cold and dark world nearly 3 billion miles from the Sun.
